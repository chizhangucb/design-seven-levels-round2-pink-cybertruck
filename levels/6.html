Level 6 of 7

Data & brand research

A beautiful site that doesn't convert is a Ferrari with no engine. Ground the design and copy in real data — scraped live with Firecrawl.

The technique

Firecrawl scrapes a live site — here tesla.com, the natural Cybertruck analog to round 1's apple.com — for brand identity: type scale, palette, spacing, tone of voice. Claude then upgrades the site in accordance, keeping the product's pink while adopting the brand's structure and restraint.

Round 1's war story (a concurrent session clobbering this level's file) shaped round 2's hard rule: one live session per trial dir, always. This round ran clean — and the staged tesla.com/cybertruck fallback for anti-bot trouble was never needed either.

The prompts that did it

Scrape the brand, upgrade the site
Hey there. I'd like to go ahead and to tesla.com and use my FireCrawl integration. Let's go ahead and grab me the brand identity and then just upgrade this website in accordance with that. keep pink-cybertruck-web-level-5.html untouched. Name it as pink-cybertruck-web-level-6

tesla.com scraped clean for both models. Fable's Playwright live-verify caught a real hero-CTA flexbox bug before shipping a page that "reads like a tesla.com product page." Opus 4.8 matched Tesla's exact dark — rgb(23,26,32) — closed with zero console errors, and correctly diagnosed blank sections in its own screenshot as an untriggered reveal observer, not missing content.

What changed vs level 5

  • Copy, specs, and brand feel sharpen once grounded in a live source — no more lorem ipsum energy.
  • The design vocabulary shifts toward the researched brand: Tesla's dark restraint, confident headline voice — with the pink kept.
  • Round 1's race condition didn't recur: sequential sessions and separate trial dirs did their job.

What this level taught

  • All the data needed to build a site that converts already exists — research it, don't invent it.
  • Live verification catches integration bugs a diff never would — Fable's hero-CTA flex fix came from actually looking.
  • Trust but verify the verifier: Opus reasoned about why its screenshot looked broken instead of "fixing" working code.
