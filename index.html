The ladder

Each level adds one technique on top of the last. Every card links to the technique, the exact prompts that drove it, and the live result from both models.

1

Grab and go

Treat Claude like a chatbot: no references, no skills, no assets. The baseline everything else improves on.
2

Screenshots & references

Show, don't tell. One reference image transfers visual intent almost losslessly — paragraphs of adjectives can't.
3

Design skills

Install packaged design expertise so the model knows what good looks like — then it stops eating crayons and starts playing the piano.
4

UI snipping

Don't re-engineer the fireplace — snip it. This round: a 21st.dev-style DottedSurface, a three.js animated dot-wave, integrated at the bottom of the page and always on.
5

AI-generated video

Generate the media you don't have. An OpenArt product film — Nano Banana image into a Kling/Seedance video — is what separates 'AI website' from 'production website'.
6

Data & brand research

A beautiful site that doesn't convert is a Ferrari with no engine. Ground the design and copy in real data — Tesla's brand system, scraped live from tesla.com with Firecrawl.
7

Design extraction

The strongest lever: point the design-language skill at teenage.engineering, extract the full design language, and rebuild your product in it. Translation, not cloning.
